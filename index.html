The Fischer Random Pawn Method

The Pawn Method is a simpler, faster way of setting up a starting position for Fischer Random Chess (aka Chess960). Unlike other methods, it doesn't require dice, coins, or a complicated process. It has been proven to select one of the 960 possible starting positions with perfect randomness. Created by Robby Walker and Tim Suzman.


First-time Setup

On the bottom of each pawn, write the following:

♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟
QBBNNK/RK/RK/R
♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟
L1L3L5L7R1R3R5R7

How to use the Pawn Method

First, randomly shuffle the white pawns in their starting row.

♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟

Look under each pawn. If the pawn says Q, B, or N, place a Queen, Bishop, or Knight in the corresponding starting square. If the pawn says K/R, ignore it for now (we'll fill in the King and Rooks at the end).

♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟ ♟
♛ ♝ ♝ ♞ ♞

IF the two bishops landed on the same color square (as they did above), we need to move one of them. Randomly select a single black pawn and look at the bottom. If it starts with L, we'll move the Left bishop, and if it starts with R, we'll move the Right bishop. The number on the black pawn tells you how many squares to the right to move that bishop (wrapping around the board if necessary). Swap the Bishop with whatever was on its new square.
